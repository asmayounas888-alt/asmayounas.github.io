About Me

Passionate about Language & Data

I'm a computational linguist specializing in natural language processing, sentiment analysis, and corpus linguistics. My work focuses on uncovering language patterns and discourse dynamics across media sources.

With expertise in corpus compilation, framing analysis, and sentiment detection, I apply computational methods to linguistic research. I'm particularly interested in comparative media analysis and how language shapes perception across Western and non-Western news sources.

My research combines linguistic theory with advanced NLP techniques including transformer models (BERT), semantic similarity analysis, and network visualization to extract meaningful insights from large textual datasets.
